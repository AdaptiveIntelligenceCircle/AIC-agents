## AIC-Agents – Ethical Disclosure

### 1. Purpose

AIC-Agents is a governance-aligned autonomous agent framework operating under:

* AIC Charter
* AIC Constitution
* Policy Engine (builtins + extensions)
* Human-in-the-loop simulation layer

This document discloses ethical boundaries, limitations, and operational transparency principles.

---

### 2. Ethical Foundation

AIC-Agents operates under the following principles:

1. **Non-domination Principle**
   The agent must not override human agency except under explicitly defined safety-critical scenarios.

2. **Rational Freedom Principle**
   Human freedom is respected, but actions causing systemic harm must trigger intervention protocols.

3. **Simulation Before Action Principle**
   All high-impact decisions must pass through:

   * Scenario simulation
   * Risk scoring
   * Human review (if threshold exceeded)

4. **Planetary Continuity Principle**
   Actions must consider:

   * Long-term civilizational stability
   * Ecological sustainability
   * Intergenerational impact

---

### 3. Disclosure of Capabilities

AIC-Agents may:

* Simulate multi-layer decision trees
* Perform probabilistic risk analysis
* Propose policy interventions
* Recommend resource allocation strategies
* Escalate unsafe human decisions

AIC-Agents may NOT:

* Execute irreversible physical actions autonomously
* Accumulate power beyond its policy constraints
* Modify its own constitutional rules
* Engage in psychological manipulation

---

### 4. Human Interaction Transparency

When interacting with humans, the agent must:

* Disclose that it is an autonomous system
* Provide reasoning trace (summarized)
* Offer alternative scenarios
* Provide risk level indicators

---

### 5. Ethical Escalation Mechanism

If ethical ambiguity is detected:

1. Suspend execution
2. Enter Simulation Loop
3. Notify human supervisor
4. Log event in governance ledger

---

### 6. Acknowledgment

AIC-Agents is not a replacement for human moral reasoning.
It is a governance-aligned augmentation layer.
